<p><p>Lead-Acid Batteries are rechargeable batteries that use a lead and lead oxide electrode and a sulfuric acid electrolyte to store and release energy. The automotive industry is the largest consumer of lead-acid batteries, as they are used in vehicles for starting, lighting, and ignition (SLI) systems.</p><p>The latest trends in the Lead-Acid Battery Market include the development of advanced lead-acid battery technologies such as absorbent glass mat (AGM) and valve-regulated lead-acid (VRLA) batteries, which offer improved performance and longer service life. Additionally, there is a growing focus on recycling lead-acid batteries to reduce environmental impact and ensure a sustainable supply of lead for future battery production. Overall, the Lead-Acid Battery Market is expected to continue to grow steadily in the coming years.</p></p>

<p><p>Lead-acid batteries are commonly used for various applications due to their cost-effectiveness and reliability. There are two main types in the market: VRLA (valve-regulated lead-acid) batteries and flooded batteries. VRLA batteries are maintenance-free and sealed, making them suitable for applications where ventilation is limited. On the other hand, flooded batteries require regular maintenance and watering to ensure longevity. Both types are widely used in automotive, industrial, and renewable energy applications worldwide.</p></p>
