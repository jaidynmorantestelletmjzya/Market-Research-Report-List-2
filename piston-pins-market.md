<p><p>Piston pins, also known as wrist pins, come in various types in the market. Semi-floating piston pins are secured in the piston with one end floating while the other end is fixed. Fully floating piston pins are completely free to move within the piston and connecting rod. Other types of piston pins may include press-fit pins or solid pins. These different types cater to different engine designs and requirements, offering various levels of performance and reliability. Each type has its own advantages and applications in the automotive industry.</p></p>
